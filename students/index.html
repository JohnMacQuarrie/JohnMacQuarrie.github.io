Doctorate

Ricardo Joel Franquiz Flores (finished 2021)

"Block Theory for Profinite Groups": the defect group of a block of a profinite group is defined and studied, giving several classifications. A Brauer Correspondence is defined for relatively pro-p groups (we've since generalized the correspondence to profinite groups). Blocks of profinite groups with cyclic defect group are classified using a profinite analogue of Brauer Tree Algebras.

Block theory and Brauer's first main theorem for profinite groups

Master's

Mattheus Pereira da Silva Aguiar (finished 2019)

"The Kurosh Subgroup Theorem for profinite groups": Mattheus read Luis Ribes' new (at the time book) "Profinite Graphs and Groups", about profinite graphs and how they help to understand constructions in the combinatorial theory of profinite groups, and taught me a bit about it. Mattheus is doing his doctorate in Brasilia with Pavel Zalesskii now.

Júlio César Magalhães Marques (finished 2019)

"The finitistic dimension conjecture": Júlio César read two articles about the intoxicatingly easy-to-state-hard-to-prove finitistic dimension conjecture. The famous paper of Igusa and Todorov wherein the Igusa-Todorov functions are defined and a new (at the time) paper of Rickard relating the conjecture to seemingly unrelated questions in the derived category. Júlio César is doing his doctorate now with Viktor Bekkert here at the UFMG, and I'm cosupervising.

João Vitor Pinto e Silva (finished 2019)

"Definable Subcategories and the Ziegler Spectrum": João Vitor read Mike Prest's book "Purity, Spectra and Localisation", about the model theoretic approach to module theory, and taught me a bit about it. João Vitor is doing his doctorate now with George Willis at the University of Newcastle, Australia.
